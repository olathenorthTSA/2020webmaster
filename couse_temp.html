2019 Design Brief
E - Communication Courses
Film Classes

History of Film

This course examines the impact of the film industry on American history and culture as advances in technology have changed the film industry over time. Students will view and write about a series of required classical films, from different genres, from silent films to the present. Research, critical thinking, analyzing, and utilization of extensive writing strategies are emphasized for all assignments and projects.

Digital Film

Students enrolled in Digital Film will produce short video productions for multiple modes of distribution. This is a fast paced class where students will acquire the skills they need to develop short films by writing scripts, directing and acting in film projects, shooting stories, editing and special effects. Students will learn how today’s Entertainment programs are produced and distributed through both broadcast channels and Internet outlets

E-News

Students enrolled in eNews will learn how today’s electronic news gathering programs are produced and distributed through both broadcast channels and Internet outlets. They will produce video and audio programs, daily, weekly and for special programming.

Video I

This course focuses on the basic concepts of video production for a variety of uses. Students integrate a variety of communications with an emphasis on broadcasting, video production and music production. Topics include analyzing the pre-production, production and post-production process, as well as explore the equipment and techniques used to develop a quality video production.

Digital Design Classes

Basic Digital Photo

This is an introduction to the concepts, tools and technology of digital imaging for photographers. Students will learn digital technology, including digital cameras and imaging software, and will use their knowledge to produce, create, and/or manipulate images for commercial and/or artistic applications.

Digital Design

Students will learn real world design skills and how to combine art & design skills with the latest in graphics software in this digital art class. This course will teach students how the elements and principles of art can enhance digital skills using current software like Photoshop and Illustrator. Through design projects, students will add tools to their artist’s toolbox for the digital design world.

Advanced Digital Photo

This course is for the dedicated digital photographer who has successfully completed the Basic Digital Photo class. This class differs from the basic course, as your photography becomes the main focus, while using programs like Photoshop to alter and improve and edit your work. This course takes considerable time outside of class to complete the photo taking requirements on a weekly basis.

Advanced Digital Design and Advertising

This is an advanced course and student should have successfully completed the beginning digital design course. Students are expected to bring considerable photoshop & Illustrator knowledge to this course. In this class, students will have a variety of real world design projects, such as posters, t-shirts, logos, etc for “real” clients. Students will continue to increase their skills in both Photoshop & Illustrator. This course is for the serious designer that wants to continue where digital design class left off. This is a great class for those needing portfolio pieces for college or job market.
