Film Courses
Taught By Mr. Lane

History of Film

This course examines the impact of the film industry on American history and culture as advances in technology have changed the film industry over time. Students will view and write about a series of required classical films, from different genres, from silent films to the present. Research, critical thinking, analyzing, and utilization of extensive writing strategies are emphasized for all assignments and projects.

Digital Film

Students enrolled in Digital Film will produce short video productions for multiple modes of distribution. This is a fast paced class where students will acquire the skills they need to develop short films by writing scripts, directing and acting in film projects, shooting stories, editing and special effects. Students will learn how today’s Entertainment programs are produced and distributed through both broadcast channels and Internet outlets

E-News

Students enrolled in eNews will learn how today’s electronic news gathering programs are produced and distributed through both broadcast channels and Internet outlets. They will produce video and audio programs, daily, weekly and for special programming.

Video I

This course focuses on the basic concepts of video production for a variety of uses. Students integrate a variety of communications with an emphasis on broadcasting, video production and music production. Topics include analyzing the pre-production, production and post-production process, as well as explore the equipment and techniques used to develop a quality video production.
